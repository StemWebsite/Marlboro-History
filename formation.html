Formation and Onwards

  • Under John W. Herbert, Marlboro became independent township under the act of New Jersey legislature on February 17, 1848
    • Consisted of portions from Freehold Township
    • First elected freeholder John W. Herbert
  • 1873- Post Office, Schools, Hotel, Stores
  • Rural place for most of its history ,and it consisted of hamlets
    • Hamlets all centered with activity at Marlboro Village, still present today
      • Intersection between Main St. and School Road
    • Dairy farms, Potato farms, Tomato farms
    • Considered one of nation’s largest grower of potatoes before WWII
    • Known for large tomato and egg industry(egg industry mushroomed during WWII)
  • Post WWII slowly became suburban
    • Improved transportation infrastructure
    • Population started to increase
    • Housing developments replaced rural amenities
    • Became a popular hub for working NYC people
  • Led to where Marlboro is today
