Dutch/English Settlement

  • Lenape restricted Dutch to Pavonia and Swedes to New Sweden
  • Dutch had influence over Monmouth Area but not full control until English took over to rule
  • 1664-Leasing of New Jersey to Sir Berkley and Carteret, Start of New Jersey’s history
  • Initially, European proprietors purchased land from the Lenape chief
  • Richard Nicolls appointed as governor of New York and New Jersey
    • Issued “Monmouth Patent” to give land to 12 immigrants
  • Unaware land went to Berkley and Carteret
    • Rule: Land purchased from patent
  • Patents ignored, land still purchased from Lenape, causing confusion
  • Around 1685, history began - Scots, Dutch, and English farmers started to settle in the area
    • Lived on isolated clearings
    • Settlers Quakers, Spoked mainly Dutch, Money British shillings
  • First settlers led by George Keith, a missionary
    • Established Topanemus, which would later become Topanemus Road
  • There were multiple sites here that would play a part in the American Revolution
  • Many churches were also built
    • St. Peter’s Episcopal Church created by Quakers who later changed to episcopal faith
    • Old Tennent Church - Presbyterian Faith built church on Gordons Corner Rd that eventually moved to Tennent
      • One of the sites that served a role in the Revolutionary War
    • Dutch established Dutch Reformed Church
  • Churches were one of the more important parts of NJ at the time
